Childcare Register rules

You must keep the children you’re looking after safe from harm and tell their parents about any health and safety risks.

You must manage children's behaviour in a suitable manner and you will not use corporal punishment.

You must supervise the children and be present at all times while you’re providing care.

You must not smoke or be under the influence of alcohol or drugs (including medication that might harm your ability to provide childcare) while looking after children.

You must be familiar with issues of child protection and procedures to safeguard children from abuse or neglect.

You must make sure that the childcare you provide is as accessible and inclusive as possible, and meets the needs of each child. You will not refuse to provide childcare or treat any child less well because of their:

  • race
  • religion
  • home language
  • family background
  • gender
  • special educational needs or disability

You must give parents:

  • a copy of your Ofsted registration certificate if they ask for it
  • information about Ofsted’s registration system for the Childcare Register
  • Ofsted’s address

If you receive a notice of suspension, you will show it to the parents of each child you look after.

You must tell Ofsted about any of the following within 14 days of the change happening:

  • a change to the address where you look after children
  • a change to your name, address or phone number
  • any significant event likely to affect your suitability to look after children

You must tell Ofsted about any of the following if they happen while you are looking after children:

  • food poisoning affecting two or more children you look after
  • any serious accident/injury to or death of any child
  • any serious accident or injury to, or the death of, any other person in the home where you look after children
  • any allegation of serious harm or abuse occurring where you work
  • any allegation of serious harm or abuse committed elsewhere by anyone who looks after children with you
